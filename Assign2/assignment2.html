Introduction to Protected Areas

A protected area is a clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means to achive the long term conservation of nature with associated ecosystem services and cultural values (IUCN Defintion 2008)1.

Protected areas provide a wide range social, environmental and economic benefits to people and communities worldwide. They provide more benefits than conserving nature as they are vital to respond to challenges, including food and water security, human health and well-being, disaster risk reduction and climate change2.

In Canada specifically, protected areas are important because they help protect wild plants and animals, while maintaining a diverse gene pool. Other benefits include providing recreation, maintaining functioning ecosystems, combatting climate change, sustaining and strengthening Indigenous knowledge, and to celebrate our natural and cultural heritage3.
